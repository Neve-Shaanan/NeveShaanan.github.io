Under the dome

The village priest, together with his sons, built a building on the site, which he inherited from his ancestors with the money they earned through the producing and selling gypsum fittings. But there was a question of how to build a dome on which to project stars, under which to gather everyone from the Bible and evolution.


A local architect tried to discourage Matyushin from building a planetarium, because he himself could not make a dome, and ordering a project was very expensive. Viktor Ivanovich was upset at first, lost sleep, and did not sleep for three days. And suddenly he remembered how in his youth his grandfathers, who were builders in Moscow, talked about the installation of the dome for Orthodox temples.


I remember all my life. The message was: "Look through your life." Maybe God removed my dream for me so that I would remember how to build a dome. I got up in the morning, checked and said: "Let them talk, and with God's help I will do it."

The family technology came down to a device reminiscent of a big compass. One rod is installed in the center of the building, the other allows you to draw an even circle. Little by little, the central rod rises, and the second one changes the radius and moves closer to the center. Former priest and carpenter Viktor Ivanovich made a wooden dome. Sawed out a part of a circle from a bar and connected it in diameter with similar constructions.


Using this technology, he made several spheres and hemispheres of different sizes, pasted maps of the starry sky on them and drew constellations. The solar system he made from wire and Christmas decorations, and the model of the Universe was made from a tennis ball and a nail.

An important part of the planetarium is a projector with which you can "show" the starry sky on the dome. Matyushin was helped to make equipment for the planetarium by Vladimir Ilyich Matsynin, a village drawing teacher, an elderly fan of astronomy from the village of Yaroslavskaya. He once also built a planetarium in his village and made the equipment himself. Vladimir Ilyich agreed to help, however, when he heard that it would be not only about astronomy, but also about God, at first he refused. But then he softened and helped. Later, Viktor Ivanovich bought a mobile planetarium on the market, and today he also uses a projector to demonstrate films on the Dome.


There are so many things which are good for illustrating the Bible and astronomy. Viktor Ivanovich shows a blue globe, one from a stationery store. The device is plugged into a socket — the ball rotates around its axle and literally floats in the air between two magnets. The kids adore such things.


The Bible says that God hung the earth on nothing. And this is on two magnets. Very visual for children — on nothing, on magnets, in the air. And there are two forces in the air — gravity and ... This is very interesting and practical for them... The Chinese invented this. Now we also have the same.
